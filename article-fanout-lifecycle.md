# The Hidden Questions Inside Every AI Answer (and How to Make Them Work for You)

## The friend who asks twenty questions

Imagine you ask a super-smart friend one question: *"What's the best restaurant near us?"*

A normal friend just answers. But your super-smart friend doesn't stop there. In their head, they quietly ask a bunch of little questions too:

- What kind of food do you feel like?
- Is it close by?
- Cheap or fancy?
- Are the reviews actually good?

Then they answer each little question and mix everything into one complete answer for you.

That's exactly what AI search does — Google's AI Mode, ChatGPT, Claude, all of them. You ask one question. It secretly asks lots of little ones. This trick has a name: **fan-out** — because one question fans out into many, like a paper fan opening up.

## You never see the little questions — and that's the problem

Here's the part that hurts marketers.

The AI never shows you its little questions. They stay hidden. And your keyword tools can't help either — these questions are made up fresh by the AI each time, so they show **zero search volume**. They're invisible to every tool you already use.

So when an AI answer recommends your competitor and skips you, you're left guessing:

- What did it actually search for?
- Which pages did it trust?
- Why wasn't ours one of them?

You can't fix what you can't see. That's the pain.

## A quick example

You ask: *"Best CRM for a small sales team."*

Behind the scenes, the AI quietly asks little questions like:

- What's a good CRM for small teams?
- How much does it cost?
- Is there a free one?
- Does it send emails for you?

Then it grabs the best pages that answer each little question and builds one single answer — with links to where it found each bit.

## Why this changes how we write content

The AI doesn't pick one page that matches your exact words. It collects bits from many pages — one for each little question.

So if your page only answers the big question, the AI can grab someone else's page for all the little ones. You get skipped.

But if your page answers the big question **and** the little questions hiding inside it, the AI has many reasons to pick you. More little questions answered = more chances to show up.

The new rule is simple:

> **Don't just answer the question. Answer all the little questions hiding inside it too.**

The only problem: you need to know what those little questions are. That's what our extension does. Open a ChatGPT or Claude conversation, click one button, and it shows you every hidden question the AI asked — plus every website it used to answer them.

But seeing the questions is just the start. Here's what you actually do with them.

## "The AI saw your page… and skipped it"

This is the insight most people miss, and it's the most valuable one.

For every answer, there are two lists:

- **Retrieved** — pages the AI found while searching. It *saw* them.
- **Cited** — pages the AI actually used in its answer. It *trusted* them.

If your page shows up as retrieved but never cited, that's not bad news — it's a gift. It means the AI already knows you exist. You don't have a visibility problem; you have a *content* problem. Something about the page — too thin, too old, wrong angle — made the AI pass. That's fixable.

The extension shows you both lists for every question. No more guessing which problem you have.

## One capture, four answers

Every conversation you capture tells you four things:

1. **The little questions** — the real sub-queries the AI ran. This is your content outline, handed to you.
2. **Who won** — which pages got cited, which got skipped, and where you landed.
3. **What kind of pages win** — does the AI cite Reddit threads? Review sites? News? Blogs? If 6 of 10 citations are Reddit, another blog post won't save you — you need to be part of the community conversation. If review sites dominate, that's where to invest.
4. **How fresh the winners are** — if cited pages are all from the last few weeks, a yearly content update won't cut it. Now you know your republish speed.

## The full loop: See → Compare → Fix → Publish → Check again

Finding the hidden questions is step one — not the whole job. The real value is running this loop, over and over:

**1. SEE**
Ask ChatGPT or Claude the questions your buyers actually ask. Capture the conversation. Now you have the real little questions and the real winning sources — no guessing.

**2. COMPARE**
Put the little questions next to your existing content. For each one, mark it:
- ✅ **Covered** — we answer this well
- 😐 **Thin** — we mention it, but a competitor answers it better
- ❌ **Missing** — we don't answer this at all

That's your content gap list, built from what the AI *actually* searched — not from a keyword tool's guess.

**3. FIX & CREATE**
- For **thin** pages: add the missing sections, FAQs, comparisons, and pricing details the little questions ask for.
- For **missing** topics: create new content — and match the *kind* of content that's winning. If community posts win, show up there. If reviews win, earn them.

**4. PUBLISH & REPUBLISH**
Ship it. And where freshness matters (the tool shows you), refresh dates, prices, and facts on your existing pages regularly. Old content quietly falls out of AI answers.

**5. CHECK AGAIN**
Two to four weeks later, run the same prompts and capture again. Type your domain into the tracker and watch two numbers: how often you're cited, and your share compared to everyone else. Went up? Do more of what you did. Still skipped? Now you know exactly which little questions to attack next.

That's the difference between a trick and a system. One capture tells you what's wrong. The loop, run monthly, is how you steadily become the page the AI picks.
